Description

Samosas are a traditional Indian dish that are served as appetizers. They are deep-fried pastries filled with potato, peas, onions, and spices, and they are usually in a triangular shape. They can be eaten as they are but people like dipping them in ketchup, chutnee, or their favorite dips.

Ingredients

For samsoas dough:

  • All-purpose flour (2 cups)
  • Grapeseed oil (0.25 cup)
  • Water (6 tablespoons)
  • Salt (0.75 teaspoon)

For potato filling:

  • 4 potatoes
  • Green peas (0.5 cup)
  • Grapeseed oil (1 tablespoon)
  • Minced ginger (1 tablespoon)
  • 2 green chili peppers
  • Lemon juice (1 teaspoon)
  • Salt (0.5 teaspoon)

Spices:

  • Cumin seeds (1 teaspoon)
  • Masala (1 teaspoon)
  • Red chili powder (1 teaspoon)

Steps

  1. Peel and boil potatoes until tender. Then, crumble them (but don't mash them).
  2. In a bowl, mix together flour, oil, and salt. Then add the water, mix it and let it rest for 20 mins.
  3. While waiting, heat a pan with oil and cumin seeds. When they begin to sizzle, add ginger and green chili peppers. Cook for 1 minute.
  4. Then mix the green peas with them and add red chili powder and masala.
  5. Stir the potatoes into this mixture of spices and sprinkle salt. Set aside to cool.
  6. Now, take the dough and knead it until smooth. Divide it into 5 pieces and roll them into balls.
  7. Flatten one ball and roll into oval shape. Cut this oval in half to form two semicircles. Each one makes one samosa.
  8. Join the edges to make a cone shape and press down to seal. Can use flour-water paste to act as a glue if needed.
  9. Fill the cone with the potato and spice mixture. Once filled, seal the opening. Repeat this process until the dough or filling runs out.
  10. Heat up oil on medim heat. Drop as many samosas that fit under the the oil into it. Cook them for 10 to 12 minutes.
  11. Once the crust is firm, increase the heat a bit and flip the samosas. Cook until golden color and take them out. Repeat for all the uncooked samosas left.
  12. Let the samosas cool off for a few minutes and serve with ketchup or chutnee. Enjoy!
